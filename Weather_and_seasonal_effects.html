Weather and seasonal effects

Important things to keep in mind:

Weather plays a significant role in everyone’s life, but it’s especially important to consider its impact on seniors. Our bodies become less capable of adjusting to temperature changes and other weather conditions as we age. That means seniors are more vulnerable to health issues caused by shifts in weather. From extreme temperatures to poor air quality, it’s essential to understand how weather impacts senior health.

  • Temperature Extremes and Seniors Health

    • One of the most significant ways weather impacts senior health is through extreme temperatures. Seniors are more sensitive to both hot and cold conditions. High temperatures can cause heat exhaustion or heat stroke during the summer. On the flip side, extremely cold temperatures can lead to hypothermia. Seniors have a harder time regulating their body temperature, so staying indoors during heatwaves or freezing weather is vital. Having access to climate-controlled spaces is key.

  • Humidity and Respiratory health.

    • Humidity can significantly impact a senior’s ability to breathe comfortably. High humidity Highh humidity levels make it harder for the body to cool down. This can exacerbate conditions like asthma or chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD). When the air is too humid, seniors may find it more difficult to breathe, which increases the risk of dehydration. Conversely, low humidity can cause dry skin, respiratory issues, and dehydration. Maintaining balanced indoor air moisture levels is important for senior health.
